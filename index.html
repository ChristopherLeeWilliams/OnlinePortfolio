My Story


My hands-on experience with computers really began part way through middle school when my parents had purchased a desktop computer as the family computer. The computer was a lower-budget build running Windows Vista and ran quite slow for my family’s liking, among other issues. Throughout the lifetime of that computer, I researched many ways to improve performance including disabling services and windows animations, deleting temporary files, updating drivers, and running native services like disk defrag. I had become the resident IT guy in our household, as well as for several of my friends, and this continued through high school and college, during which I fixed and built several computers.

The start of my college career began at Monterey Peninsula College in the fall semester of 2013. I went in to pursue an Associate’s degree before transferring, to allow myself time to feel out what career I wanted to pursue. I knew I wanted to work with computers but wasn’t sure in what capacity. It wasn’t until my second semester, in which I took a Java course, that I knew which path I wanted to take. The Java course, CSIS 10A, completely submerged me in coding and how to think through an Object-Oriented programming lens. It had us explore coding techniques like loops and recursion, data structures, input and output operations, inheritance, and more fundamentals. Peer coding and code review was incorporated into our weekly lessons, which I did not experience much in my education after this class, making it all the more important and memorable. While the class was difficult as an introduction to programming, I attribute much of the reason I pursued this path to the professor at the time, Brian Schlining.

During the rest of my education at MPC I took various courses covering topics from computer hardware to coding in MATLAB. I started working at Water City Sports and Roller Hockey as a skate guard and briefly took part in an internship at Desert Star Systems in which I worked towards developing a tool in C# to calculate a location’s local sunrise and sunset time given a GPS coordinate.

After graduating from MPC with an AA in computer information systems, I transferred to California State University Monterey Bay to pursue my bachelor’s degree with a focus in software engineering. During the first semester at CSUMB I had my second major milestone with regards to career direction: I took an internet programming course taught by Jason Henderson. Up to that point, creating a client-facing application seemed like something that had an incredible amount of overhead and would likely only reach a niche subsection of the general population. Websites and web apps however seemed like much more accessible utilities that could be client-facing and useful in a single day’s work, for the simpler projects at least. With nearly every facet of daily life expanding to incorporate web technologies in some capacity, web development seemed like a must.

There wasn’t a formal web development path to take at CSUMB, however I opted for all my projects to be web-based where I could. Some projects I took part in included an online audio editor and converter in Python using Flask, a virtual computer building and compatibility checking tool in PHP, a vacation planner tool in JavaScript that utilized MongoDB and Express.js, and finally my capstone project which also led to my first job in the industry, the Parabug Estimate Solutions Tool (PEST).

The PEST web application was developed to streamline the customer job request system for Parabug, a company specializing in agricultural pest management through the use of biologicals deployed via drone. The PEST application did this by allowing customers to draw out an anti-pest deployment map through a Google Maps interface while being able to include other relevant information via text input. The map data and user input were then automatically emailed to Parabug as a kml file and pdf file upon the customer submitting the form.

My last semester at CSUMB concluded in April of 2019 and I received my B.S. in Computer Science. While the capstone class had ended, the PEST project had room for improvement as well as several features/reach goals outside of the scope of the original capstone. Under the guidance of Chandler Bennett, our capstone mentor and the founder of Parabug, I assumed the role of web developer for the project and have since added a host of new features from map overlay imports (kmz, kml, shp, GeoJson, GeoTiff) to custom algorithms involving geographic geometries (concave hull generation, “tileization”).

Alongside my work at Parabug, I took up the manager position at Water City Sports and Roller Hockey shortly after graduating. As a manager I was tasked with leading a team through the setup, hosting, and cleaning for public skate sessions, private parties, and various other events which generally involved between 100 to 200 people. Water City’s website was also quite dated and in need of updates, so I rebuilt it on Wix, following SEO best practices. I recognized the effectiveness of an up to date and search engine optimized website for a business firsthand, with a notable increase in new customers and a reduction in reported confusion with pricing and timings following the update.

Since then, I have taken part in the development and maintenance of websites and web applications across several different industries. One of the more notable projects of these was the Plantlogic web application developed for Merrill Farms, a Salinas-based agriculture employer and grower. Plantlogic is a Dockerized Angular 12 web application that provides comprehensive record keeping and data management tools for all parties involved in the process of growing agriculture. It started as a capstone project, similar to the PEST application, and I took over its development after the capstone portion had concluded. During my work on the project I added several new custom user roles and permissions, halved load times and the size of data transfer across all pages through optimizing database queries, created a user-customizable csv templating system for exporting data, expanded application compatibility for mobile devices, and more.

I now live in San Diego and am involved in the development and maintenance of 2 previously mentioned web apps, PEST and Plantlogic, as well as 2 other websites. I am looking for a more full-time position in which I can both further my experience as a developer and feel that the work I contribute is making a meaningful impact.
